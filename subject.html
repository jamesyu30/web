Lions

Where are they found?

Lions are mostly found here: West and Central Africa (Angola, Benin, Burkina Faso, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, The Democratic Republic of the Congo, Gabon, Niger, Nigeria, Senegal), East Africa (Ethiopia, Kenya, Malawi, Mozambique, Rwanda, Somalia, South Sudan, Sudan, Tanzania, Uganda, Zambia, and Zimbabwe), and Southern Africa (Botswana, Namibia, South Africa, and Swaziland). They mostly live on the continent of Africa, however, there are some endangered Asiatic lions living in India.

What do they eat?

Lions are carnivorous, so they eat meat. They are also the apex predator, so they eat a lot of many different species. They mostly eat: buffaloes, rhinoceros, younger elephants, hippopotamus, zebras, giraffes, crocodiles, wild hogs, and antelopes.

How long do they sleep?

Male lions get 18 to 20 hours of sleep a day while female lions sleep for 15 to 18 hours
